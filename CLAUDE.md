## What's Working
<!-- Sync: 2026-04-02 -->
- **Short-form copy format** — The new campaigns (86, 118) use 60-80 word emails and achieve 4.5-4.7% reply rates vs 0.3-2.0% on the old campaigns
- **Google-to-Google sending** — Best deliverability and reply rates
- **Social proof lead** — Opening with "Brands like Nickelodeon, Disney and LEGO use Tongal" gets engagement
- **Soft CTA** — "Worth exploring for {Company}?" generates positive responses
- **Enterprise targeting** — Positive replies from SVP at Crunchyroll, Creative Director at Vanta

## What's Not Working
<!-- Sync: 2026-04-02 -->
- **Subject lines are formulaic** — Every email uses "{Company} + video production" pattern. No variety, feels templated
- **OOO inflation** — Majority of "replies" are out-of-office auto-replies, inflating metrics. Real reply rate likely ~1-2%
- **Case study mismatch** — Same 3 case studies used regardless of industry (entertainment targets get BetterHelp, tech targets get Nickelodeon)
- **Growth campaign had broken variables** — Evans Transportation email showed literal "Case Study 1, Case Study 2 and Case Study 3" instead of resolved names
- **Growth campaign copy too long** — 200+ words, multiple paragraphs. Prospects don't read this
- **Wrong persona targeting** — BDRs, Social Studies department heads, people not in marketing/content
- **Lead freshness** — Several replies from people who've left the company (ClickUp, MrBeast, Warner Music Group)
- **Outlook sending** — 0.3% reply rate, now correctly paused
